Ginkgo Biloba is one of the most extensively researched botanicals for tinnitus. Antioxidant Protection

Green Tea Extract

St. John's Wort
Mood and Nerve Support

St. John's Wort is included not as a direct tinnitus treatment but because chronic tinnitus is closely associated with elevated anxiety and disturbed sleep, which in turn worsen symptom perception. Bacopa Monnieri

Bacopa is an Ayurvedic herb with a significant body of research supporting its role in improving neuroplasticity and reducing neuroinflammation. Magnesium
Cochlear Protectant

Magnesium deficiency has been directly linked to increased tinnitus severity and susceptibility to noise-induced hearing loss in controlled studies. Research suggests magnesium protects cochlear hair cells by blocking the overactivation of NMDA receptors during acoustic trauma, and that supplementation may reduce both the intensity and frequency of tinnitus episodes.

Zinc
Immune and Hair Cell Support

The cochlea is one of the highest-zinc-concentration tissues in the human body, and research shows a significant correlation between zinc deficiency and tinnitus prevalence. Studies indicate zinc supplementation reduces tinnitus loudness in deficient patients and supports the immune function of the inner ear, which is vulnerable to viral and inflammatory damage.
